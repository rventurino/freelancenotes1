Section 1: Should I freelance?

A.)Is freelancing a good fit for me? -self motivated? yes - do i need structure to succeed? no, I have not had a work schedule in my 3+ years with uber

B.) What's the market like? $21.6 billion - roughly 24 million businesses in USA - roughly 30% of those don't have a site or an outdated site - If 30% of them need work done, that is 7 million businesses - 7 million * $3,000 = 21 billion dollar market

Section 2: How to Stand Out

A.) Disconnect between devs and people consuming services - Devs have trouble connecting with people - Understand that once you're a freelancer you are not a developer, you are a problem solver

B.) Problem Solving - Coding skills mean nothing - Restaurant owner wants their pictures of their place up, not to know what stack you use - Address business needs

C.) CLOSE! - being a problem solver lends itself to sales - a lot of people aren't comfortable selling - anyone can be good at this but it will take practice - you can sell your services, you need to do it - you must sell your services

Section 3: How to SELL

A.) Initial Meeting - Identify business problems - Ask about their clientele, online ordering, goals as a business, etc. - Identify how the business stands out - builds rapport with the business owner

B.) Proposal - explain how you can address problems presented in step A - don't do this with too much jargon

C.) CLOSE - the contract MUST include technical details - often this will happen quite fast and can be within the conversation

Tips: Treat this with respect and seriousness, do not take calls when you're driving down the road. You need to take notes and be extremely attentive and detail oriented. You're dealing with someone else's business.

Section 4: WHERE to SELL

A.) Network - go to network events - most people will be other devs here - try to find places where there are will actually be small business owners, interested in talking about business

B.) 3rd party: make less but high volume of customers

c.) promote your own site on adwords. Avoid management services
